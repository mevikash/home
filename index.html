About Me

I am a Ph.D. researcher in Materials Science & Engineering at The University of Texas at Austin, working in Prof. Narayana Aluru's Multiscale Engineering, Mathematics and Sciences Group. My current research focuses on Li isotope separation using Nuclear Quantum Effects, AIMD, Machine Learning, and Path Integral Methods.

I completed my B.S. and M.S. in Chemistry from IIT Kanpur, where I specialized in surface science, 2D COFs, MOFs, molecular electronics, AFM, C-AFM, and XPS under Prof. T. G. Gopakumar.

Beyond research, I am a passionate full-stack developer with experience in React.js, Next.js, Angular, Electron.js, and mobile development — bridging the worlds of computational science and modern software engineering.

Developed ultra-thin molecular films via drop casting with Pd & Cu dopants on HOPG surfaces. Formed semiconducting surface-confined metal–organic networks (SMONs). Analyzed using C-AFM, XPS, and DFT simulations (QuantumWise ATK).
